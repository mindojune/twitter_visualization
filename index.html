#MAGA and #MeToo: A Tale of Two Hashtags

#MAGA and # MeToo are two hashtags at odds with each other that represent the opposite ends of the political spectrum in 2010s America. While #MeToo was very explicitly a Twitter-borne movement, #MAGA (acronym for #MakeAmericaGreatAgain) is a slogan of a presidentical campaign and resulting presidency very much driven by the Twitter horn.

This visualization tracks the history of each hashtag's popularity over the course of time, as well as seeing how other hashtags come into play in a particular time period specified by you.

Event Significance

Comparing the #MeToo trends to #MAGA’s in the October 2017-April 2018 period, one can see that #MeToo popularity is probably a lot more event-dependent than #MAGA with more extreme peaks.

This however is less the case with #MAGA (or, in this case, #MakeAmericaGreatAgain)’s trends in 2015 when it started becoming more commonly used in Twitter.

Beside Likes, Reweets, and Replies, we also look at Total and Ratio of Tweets.

Total = Likes + Retweets + Replies

Total can be thought of as an indicator of a tweet's overall popularity.

Ratio = (Likes + Retweets) / Replies

Ratio indicates the degree to which people agree with a particular tweet. If the ratio of a tweet is low, it means that more people replied (presumably to argue or berate) than liked/retweeted, meaning that the tweet has not been received well.
